Drought-proofing

This is at the top of the list for a reason, especially in older homes with old windows and doors. Poorly drought-proofed home will waste a lot of energy trying to heat up a home, during cold and windy season. Cheapest option is to DIY jobs, just by buying and installing window drought stopper seals around all windows, you will make your home more energy efficient.

Open chimney is a major source of heat loss, that should be plugged securely if not in use. This alone would save you approx. €60 per year in heating bills.

Something as simple as closing doors of rooms reduces drought through out the house.

Smart use of home appliances

Some appliances are very heavy on electricity consumption and their usage should not be wasted, have some of the highest consumption appliances examples below. Also to put it in perspective, jump to our page that shows just how much energy and money appliances consume here


  • Thumble dryers are very electricity hungry machines, so try to avoid using it on warm and dry days and wire hang your clothes outside.
  • Washing machines are also heavy on energy, mainly to heat the water, so washing your less dirty clothes in lower Temperature programs will help you reduce your bill.
  • Dishwasher, same as washing machine, uses most of energy to heat the water, so setting it to lower Temperature and turning it on only when it's full will save you money.
  • Kettle is another energy heavy appliance, so you should try to only boil the water you need at that time and avoid boiling full kettle just for 1 cup of tea.

Temperature, timers and controls

Set the times that your heating comes on and off so that it fits with your daily routine. Radiators will continue to heat your home for some time after the heating is turned off. So try this, turn on your heating 30 minutes before you need it and turn it off 30 minutes before you don't need it anymore. Smart thermostats comes in handy when you can just control your heating from your phone and there's even a €700 grant available from government grants mentioned at the beginning here

Turning down your thermostat and putting a jumper on instead of walking with a t-shirt in the middle of winter will also fill your wallet with saved money from heating bills. On average turning down thermostat by 1°C could save you close to 10% on heating

Remember when your dad used to give out for leaving the lights on when you were a kid? Yeah, forgeting to turn them off when you leave the room can build up in costs, especially if you use old type of lights. Which is a great investment to upgrade all light bulbs to more efficient ones like LED type.
