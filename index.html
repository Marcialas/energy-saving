Before you start

First you have to understand your energy bill and your usage. If you Click here Bord Gais will help you understand it in detail. Most utility providers bills has same structure, so know one, know them all.

One of the ways to save in the long term, is to retrofit your home, but it involves major investment, we are talking tens of thousands euro to fully retrofit a home. If you have cash to spare, it might be a good idea to invest in your family home, luckily enough government has a variety of grants available to help you cover the costs.
But bear in mind that you have to pay in full to contractor and then apply for the grant.
More information on grants available can be found here.

Drought-proofing

This is at the top of the list for a reason, especially in older homes with old windows and doors. Poorly drought-proofed home will waste a lot of energy trying to heat up a home, during cold and windy season. Cheapest option is to DIY jobs, just by buying and installing window drought stopper seals around all windows, you will make your home more energy efficient.

Open chimney is a major source of heat loss, that should be plugged securely if not in use. This alone would save you approx. €60 per year in heating bills.

Something as simple as closing doors of rooms reduces drought through out the house.

Temperature, timers and controls

Set the times that your heating comes on and off so that it fits with your daily routine. Radiators will continue to heat your home for some time after the heating is turned off. So try this, turn on your heating 30 minutes before you need it and turn it off 30 minutes before you don't need it anymore. Smart thermostats comes in handy when you can just control your heating from your phone and there's even a €700 grant available from government grants mentioned here

Turning down your thermostat and putting a jumper on instead of walking with a t-shirt in the middle of winter will also fill your wallet with saved money from heating bills. On average turning down thermostat by 1°C could save you close to 10% on heating
